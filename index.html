About Me

I am Ahmad Tayeb, a Ph.D. holder in Computer Science from Florida State University, USA. My academic journey began at King Abdulaziz University in Saudi Arabia, where I earned a Bachelor's degree in Information Technology. I then pursued a Master of Science in Information Sciences and Technologies at the B. Thomas Golisano College of Computing and Information Sciences at the Rochester Institute of Technology, USA.

I have served as a lecturer at King Abdulaziz University in the Faculty of Computing and Information Technology, where I took on various roles, including course coordinator, director of the Academic Support Unit, and supervisor of several academic clubs and units. I developed systems for exam and student management, contributing to the enhancement of academic processes. My teaching experience includes courses such as Computer Skills, Problem Solving, Object-Oriented Programming, and Android Application Development.

During my time at Florida State University, I worked as a Teaching Assistant, delivering recitation lectures for the Software Engineering class. I was also a Graduate Research Assistant, engaging in research on software development video tutorials, code review analysis, and non-inclusive language in software-related materials. Additionally, I contributed to a project for the Florida Department of Transportation, where I was involved in evaluating, maintaining, and evolving the traffic polling and analysis system through literature reviews, surveys, system analysis, and documentation.

My expertise spans Software Engineering, Web and Mobile Applications, Human-Computer Interaction, and Natural Language Processing applications. My research has been published in esteemed journals and conferences, and I was honored with the prestigious IEEE TCSE Distinguished Paper Award at the International Conference on Software Maintenance and Evolution (ICSME) 2024 for my work on the impact of generative AI tools on developers' learning and problem-solving practices.
